Types of Fixed Spout Faucets

One-Handle Faucets

One-handle faucets use one handle to control both the volume of water flow and the temperature. Moenâ€™s standard cylinder or Duralast™ cartridges aid in providing precise temperature control.

Hot and cold water lines connect at a single point beneath the faucet. An escutcheon can be used for situations where an owner wants to convert from a two-handle to a one-handle configuration.

Two-Handle Faucets

Two-handle faucets are the most basic kitchen faucets. One handle controls hot water and the other controls cold. Temperature can be adjusted by controlling the volume of water flowing through each water line with the handles.

Hot and cold water lines connect into the base of each side of the faucet.
