Product Overview

Anatomy of a Valve

Every shower has a valve that acts like an engine–shifting flow, regulating temperature and driving performance, and there are many options of design, functionality and operation suited to different applications.

The valve is placed behind the wall and connects directly to the hot and cold water lines to regulate flow and temperature. A cartridge inserted into the valve connects to the external trim handles to control the mixture of hot and cold water and flow rate through the faucet based on the handle position.

With any Moen valve, you can be certain of unsurpassed safety, comfort and reliability since our valves meet applicable pressure-balancing or thermostatic safety codes and eliminate worry about hot or cold water surges. Plus you can rely on our quality construction and dependable operation since each valve is backed by our Limited Lifetime Warranty.

Valve Basics

Terminology

There are a variety of terms attached to valve function– here are quick- reference definitions of a few of the most common:

Pressure-balancing – Protects users from “shower shock” due to a sudden change in either hot- or cold-water pressure elsewhere in the plumbing system (i.e., toilet flush). Pressure Balancing valves are designed to detect the pressure drop and automatically adjust the flow of water to maintain a consistent water temperature.

Thermostatic – These valves use a device to monitor temperature and keep the temperature consistent even as hot water levels drop in a hot water heater tank.

Cycling Valve – Turns on and off in the cold position; cycles through to the hot setting based on desired temperature.

Volume Control – Maintains consistent water-flow rate as other plumbing fixtures connected to the line are in use.

ioDigital® Vertical Spa Valve

ioDigital® gives the user precise control of temperature, flow and custom presets through a digitally controlled valve unit.

How it is used:

Showers ranging from single-head to vertical spa. Valve unit comes with either one or four outlets. Hot and cold inlets feed water through valve unit.

Installation:

The plumber need only attach the water lines to the valve unit and plug it into a nearby 110-volt electrical outlet.

Benefits:

  • Digital valve control for added precision – flow and volume.

ExactTemp® ¾” Thermostatic Valve

The ExactTemp valve combines both pressure-balancing and thermostatic technologies. It uses the 1248 cartridge.

How it is used:

Showers ranging from single-head and faucet to vertical spa when combined with a transfer valve(s). Hot and cold inlets feed water through valve unit.

Installation:

Accommodates standard ½” connections (both IPS and CC).

Benefits:

  • Set maximum water temperature and temperature memory.
  • Installation works with common household plumbing.
  • Meets pressure-balancing safety codes.
  • Integrates with Moen M•PACT common valve system.
  • ExactTemp® to allow faucet replacement without replacing any plumbing.

Moentrol® Valve

The Moentrol® valve has a pressure-balancing mechanism. It uses the 1225 cartridge and can adapt to two- or three-function operation as needed for the addition of hand-showers or for vertical spas.

How it is used:

One-handle faucets and shower/tub applications. Separate hot and cold inlets and single outlet. Additional functionality with the two- or three-function Moentrol valve.

INSTALLATION:

Set temperature limit at install. Accommodates standard ½” connections (both IPS and CC). Integrated.

Quick Fix:

Most servicing can be done without turning off the water at the main by simply removing the cartridge.

Benefits:

  • Set maximum water temperature and temperature memory.
  • Installation works with common household plumbing.
  • Meets pressure-balancing safety codes.
  • Integrates with Moen M•PACT common valve system.
  • ExactTemp® to allow faucet replacement without replacing any plumbing.

Posi-Temp Valve

Also called a “cycling valve,” the Posi-Temp® Valve is a downstream design that uses the 1222 high-flow cartridge

How it is used:

Shower and tub applications. Expandable to multi-outlet with addition of a transfer valve.

Installation:

Flexible installation options with copper, CPVC, PEX and IPS fitting options. Quarter turn-stops prevent the necessity to shut off water at the main line.

Quick Fix:

Most servicing can be done without turning off the water at the main by simply removing the cartridge.

Benefits:

  • Flow reversibility.
  • Volume control &amo; temperature memory.
  • Set maximum temperature upon installation.
  • Vertical spa compatibility.
